02

Four assistants. One boss: you.

@cos
Chief of Staff
Your daily driver. The 1–3 things that actually matter today, meeting prep with talking points, a weekly review every Friday.
"Skip the 14:00, send the deck instead. Your quarter goal lives in the 15:30."
@coach
Career Coach
Holds the long arc. Maps the gap to your next role, builds the promotion case from logged evidence, runs the quarterly check-in against your vision.
"Three logged wins this quarter. Your boss has seen one. Let's fix the other two."
@ghost
Ghost
Writes in your voice, learned from your real emails. Every text is a draft. Sending is always, only, yours.
"Kept it firmer than usual. He ignored the first two deadlines."
@life
Life Planner
The half of the vision that isn't a job title. Family dates, energy, boundaries. Argues the life side of every big trade-off, and ties go to life.
"That offsite collides with the school play. Here's the alternative before you say yes."

04

The vision is wired, not framed

10 years
The ordinary Tuesday you chose. Written in your own words.
5 years
The milestones that must be true halfway.
This year
What has to happen in the next 12 months.
This quarter
3–5 goals. Nothing else.

Every quarter, the coach walks the cascade top-down: is the picture still true, is the milestone on track, did the last 13 weeks move it? Most people write a vision once and lose the file. Yours has a staff.

05

Nothing leaves your machine

  • Your answers live in plain text files on your disk, excluded from git. Updates can never upload them.
  • No telemetry. No analytics, no phone-home, no vendor peeking at your files.
  • The only external call is Claude itself, under your own account.
  • Career ambitions and family context are radioactive. Tuesday treats them that way: the family file is never quoted into work contexts, ever.
# .gitignore — the whole privacy model, visible

DRAFTS/* # your words, never committed
