A personal system for executives

Ten years from now: what does a normal Tuesday look like?

Not the gala day, but the ordinary Tuesday that happens five hundred times a decade and quietly is the decade. Tuesday is a private AI staff on your own computer: it learns who you are, writes in your voice, guards your evenings, and keeps a ten-year plan wired to what you do this week. The wave rearranging careers does not care whether you paddle. This is a board.

The ghost: AI that stops sounding like AI

A model left alone writes the most probable sentence, and the most probable sentence belongs to nobody; the ghost studies your real sent mail and writes like you, in every language you use.

Four assistants. One boss: you.

@cos
Chief of Staff
Reads the quarter plan before it reads your calendar, which is why its idea of "important" survives contact with your inbox.
"Skip the 14:00, send the deck instead. Your quarter goal lives in the 15:30."
@coach
Career Coach
Holds the ten-year arc so you do not have to keep it in working memory, and builds the promotion case strictly from logged evidence.
"Three logged wins this quarter. Your boss has seen one. Let's fix the other two."
@ghost
Ghost
Writes in your voice, learned from your real sent mail, and never sends: every text is a draft with your name on the send button.
"Kept it firmer than usual. He ignored the first two deadlines."
@life
Life Planner
Defends the school play against the offsite before you have given the slot away. Ties go to life; that rule is written down.
"That offsite collides with the school play. Here's the alternative before you say yes."

08

Nothing leaves. Nobody gets in.

Your ambitions, your family, your plan: plain text on your own disk, with no server anywhere that could be breached, subpoenaed or sold.

1
Your disk, your files. Plain text you can open, back up or delete yourself.
2
The git blockade. Updates flow in; nothing flows out, even by accident.
3
Zero telemetry. No backend, no phone-home. What does not exist cannot leak.
4
Discretion rules. The family file never appears in a work context, ever.
5
One guarded line out. Claude, under your own account, and you see every word.
